Passphrases vs. Passwords

Traditional passwords like P@ssw0rd1! are short and hard to remember, but easy for GPU computers to crack. A passphrase consisting of four or more random, unrelated words (e.g. correct horse battery staple) is extremely difficult for computer algorithms to crack, but very easy for a human to remember.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-Factor Authentication requires you to present two or more credentials (something you know, something you have, or something you are) before gaining access. If a hacker intercepts your password, they still cannot access your accounts without your mobile authenticator code or hardware key.

Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a social engineering attack where hackers impersonate trust companies via email to steal login keys. Before typing your master password anywhere, check the URL domain carefully. Real organizations will never email you direct links to change passwords without a user-initiated request.

Use a Password Manager

Humans are bad at remembering unique passwords for 100+ services, leading to password reuse. Password managers act as an encrypted vault that generates, stores, and fills random logins. You only need to remember one complex Master Password to access all other accounts safely.
